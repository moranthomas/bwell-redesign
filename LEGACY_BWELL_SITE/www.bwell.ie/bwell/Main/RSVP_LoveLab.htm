At the Love Lab - The mathematics of Love & Divorce
      
      
Whoever would have thought that Maths would play such a large role in your love life, not I! But according to Dr. John Gottman of University of Washington, dubbed the ‘Love Lab’ a mathematician and Psychologist who married his two backgrounds to understand Psycho-physiology, in English, this is "the study of the body and the face, voice and emotion in relationships’. When asked to re-call an argument newlyweds were observed through electrodes, sensors and videotape (not your average way to have fun on a Saturday night!) the ‘Love Lab’ could predict with 91% accuracy from one 15-minute conversation if you will get divorced or not.

How does he do this, well it’s a numbers game, Gottman would wire up his newlyweds to read their blood pressure, heart rate and temperature, then they would videotape the couple as they were asked to reenact a recent argument, and then to recall the relationship history, this is where it gets all scientific on us. Gottman divided up all the possible emotions you can feel in any argument such as disgust, which was given a numerical value of 1, contempt is 2, anger is 7, defensiveness is 10, whining is 11 and so on and then the videotape was stopped and read down to milliseconds and all this was put into a complex equation that gives this highly accurate reading on whether your relationship will flourish or end in divorce.

In terms of the negative emotions he points to four very toxic and pernicious relationship deal breakers which are:

• Criticism – ‘you always do that’
• Contempt – ‘you are always right, darling’.
• Defensiveness – ‘you made me so angry’
• Stonewalling – acting as if you don’t exist, or if you don’t hear or see them

The filmed footage to the untrained eye may seem fairly innocuous, but for Gottman he could see in a millisecond of slowed footage the slight eye roll, showing contempt and the data from the electrodes could show the husband’s heart pounding as the wife pours on heavy a sarcastic remark.

On a positive note, the studies were also interested in what made a marriage work, and in couples who engaged in more positives comments than negatives in an argument, they created a magic ratio that would weather out emotional storms. This magic ratio of five positives to every one negative is the core to a healthy relationship.
      
      
      
      
      
You may think that doesn’t sound too hard, but let’s just have a re-cap on any given day, if you said to your hubbie to be, “I can’t believe you forgot to take out the bins, get the milk, pick up my tuxes for the grooms party….you get the message, you now need to give five positive comments back to him to redress the balance.

Building and maintaining a good friendship is another marriage builder, it builds up your bank of ‘giving them the benefit of the doubt’ which is essential in your marriage. Add humour to this recipe and it will re-frame even the trickiest situation, this does not mean laughing in your partners face, but a well timed witty-crack something us Irish our specialists at can break mounting tension. When you think back on most arguments they are often comical. It is our reaction to the other person and vice versa that makes it feel like emotional wound, when you think of most good comedians they are the ones who pick out our everyday human foibles.

Happy couples also acknowledged that they were sorry for hurting each others feelings and this empathy goes a lot way to repair any damage done by negative words and gestures.
So for lasting love, keep working on your friendship and always remember the gap, the gap is the choice that you have to say something negative or something positive that will nurture and protect your relationship rather than rip it apart. You do not want a Pyrrhic victory, that today’s’ ‘win' has a devastating long term loss, your marriage.
